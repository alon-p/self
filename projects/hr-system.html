HR Management System

A no-nonsense back-office system for a workers' union, turning monthly piles of spreadsheets and printed paper into a single, automated workflow.

Stack
Python/Django (Django Admin) · PostgreSQL · Celery · ZenDesk · Mailchimp
Role
Primary developer, leading a junior
Context
A workers' union office managing members and their benefits

The Challenge

The client is a workers' union office responsible for its members and the benefits they're entitled to. Their process was almost entirely manual: lots of hand-entered data, printed paper, and spreadsheets scattered everywhere. Member information lived across many Excel files, which made it slow to work with and easy for things to fall out of date.

Every month brought the same heavy task: a fresh Excel export of several thousand workers and their data that had to be reconciled into the system: updating each member's status, filling in missing information, and updating everything that depended on it.

The Approach

I built the system on Django Admin as the back office, with a monthly bulk-import workflow at its core, plus integrations to the tools the union already used (ZenDesk, Mailchimp) and scheduled automation to keep the monthly cycle on track.

Key Technical Decisions

Django Admin as the foundation: a deliberate choice, not a shortcut

For a back-office tool used by just a few staff members, Django Admin is fast and simple to stand up and does the job well. The client needed something that works without paying for a designer and a UX expert to build a bespoke interface. Spending that budget on functionality instead of a custom UI was the right call for this client.

Bulk Excel import built to reconcile, not just insert

The monthly upload of a few thousand records doesn't just add rows: it updates members' statuses, completes missing fields, and updates related data. The import had to handle real, evolving data each month.

Scheduled automation with Celery

A recurring reminder to upload the monthly Excel data, so the critical monthly step doesn't get forgotten, replacing a manual "remember to do this" with an automatic nudge.

Integrations

  • ZenDesk ran the union's task management, so I built a two-way integration: new workers are created in ZenDesk from our app, and when a worker opens a ticket in ZenDesk, their details are pulled from our system. Support and member data stay in sync.
  • Mailchimp receives member details pushed from our app, keeping the email lists current for communications.

My Role

I was mostly the sole developer, and also led a junior developer who worked on the same codebase, sharing the work and mentoring along the way. I owned the data model, the import workflow, the integrations, and the scheduled automation.

Results & Impact

  • Replaced a paper-and-spreadsheet process with a single system the union's staff work in directly.
  • A monthly reconciliation of several thousand member records became a repeatable import instead of manual data wrangling.
  • ZenDesk and Mailchimp stay in sync automatically, so support and email lists reflect current member data without manual copying.
  • A scheduled reminder keeps the monthly data cycle from slipping.

Reflection

Django Admin gets underrated. It isn't shiny React-and-Tailwind work, but for a back-office tool with a handful of users, it does a genuinely good job, fast. Matching the tool to the client's actual needs and budget mattered more than building something flashy.
